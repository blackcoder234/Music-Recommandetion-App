1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and deliver information to website owners.

1.1 How Cookies Work

When you visit Beatify, we may place cookies on your device. These cookies help us:

  • Remember your login status and preferences
  • Understand how you use our service
  • Personalize your music recommendations
  • Deliver relevant content and features
  • Analyze site performance and usage patterns

1.2 Cookies vs. Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as:

  • Web Beacons (Pixels): Tiny graphics embedded in emails or web pages to track activity
  • Local Storage: Data stored directly in your browser for offline functionality
  • Session Storage: Temporary data that expires when you close your browser
  • Device Fingerprinting: Collecting device characteristics for identification

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

2.1 Essential Functions

  • Authentication: Keep you logged in across browsing sessions
  • Security: Detect and prevent fraudulent activity
  • Session Management: Maintain your playback state and queue
  • Load Balancing: Distribute traffic across our servers

2.2 Personalization

  • Recommendations: Analyze your listening history to suggest music
  • Preferences: Remember your audio quality, language, and theme settings
  • Playlists: Store your recently played tracks and custom playlists

2.3 Analytics & Performance

  • Usage Analytics: Understand which features are most popular
  • Error Tracking: Identify and fix technical issues
  • A/B Testing: Test new features with select users
  • Performance Monitoring: Measure load times and optimize speed

2.4 Advertising (Free Tier)

For users on the free tier, we use cookies to deliver relevant advertisements:

  • Show ads based on your interests and listening habits
  • Limit the number of times you see the same ad
  • Measure ad effectiveness and campaign performance

Note: Premium subscribers do not receive ads and are not subject to advertising cookies.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Performance Analyze site usage, measure performance, and track errors 2 years No

3.1 Session vs. Persistent Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. Used for authentication and navigation.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period (or until you delete them). Used for preferences and analytics.

3.2 First-Party vs. Third-Party

  • First-Party Cookies: Set by Beatify directly to provide core functionality and personalization.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services (analytics, advertising, payment processors) we integrate with.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Beatify integrates with trusted third-party services that may set their own cookies. These services are subject to their own privacy policies and cookie practices.

4.1 Analytics Partners

  • Google Analytics: Tracks user behavior, demographics, and site performance
  • Mixpanel: Measures user engagement and feature adoption
  • Amplitude: Analyzes product usage and retention metrics

4.2 Advertising Partners

For free-tier users, we work with advertising networks:

  • Google Ads: Delivers targeted display and video ads
  • Facebook Pixel: Tracks conversions and retargets users
  • Ad Exchanges: Real-time bidding platforms for programmatic ads

4.3 Payment & Support

  • Stripe & PayPal: Process payments securely
  • Zendesk & Intercom: Provide customer support and live chat

Note: We do not control third-party cookies. To learn more about how these services use cookies, please review their respective privacy policies.

5. Managing Cookies

You have several options to control or disable cookies based on your preferences.

5.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to manage cookies through their settings:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies

5.2 Cookie Preferences Center

Use our Cookie Preferences tool below to customize which types of cookies you allow. Note that disabling certain cookies may limit functionality.

5.3 Opt-Out Tools

To opt out of specific tracking and advertising cookies, visit:

  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) Opt-Out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) Opt-Out
  • Your Online Choices (EU)

5.4 Mobile Devices

To manage tracking on mobile devices, adjust your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking → Limit Ad Tracking
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization

Important: Blocking or deleting cookies may impact your Beatify experience. Some features may not function properly, and you may lose personalized settings.

6. Cookie Consent

6.1 How We Obtain Consent

When you first visit Beatify, we display a cookie consent banner that explains our use of cookies. You can:

  • Accept All: Consent to all types of cookies
  • Reject Non-Essential: Allow only strictly necessary cookies
  • Customize: Select specific cookie categories to enable

6.2 Withdrawing Consent

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by accessing the Cookie Preferences Center or adjusting your browser settings. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before withdrawal.

6.3 EU & UK Users

If you're in the European Union or United Kingdom, we comply with the ePrivacy Directive and GDPR. We will not use non-essential cookies without your explicit consent.

7. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, or legal requirements. When we make significant changes, we'll notify you through:

  • A prominent notice on our website
  • An in-app notification
  • Email to your registered address

The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when it was last revised. Your continued use of Beatify after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
